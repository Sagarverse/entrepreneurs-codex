# Privacy Policy — The Entrepreneur's Codex

**Last updated:** 2026-07-06

The Entrepreneur's Codex ("the app", "we", "us") is built around a
single promise: **what you write is yours, and it stays on your
device.**

## What we collect

Nothing.

## What is stored on your device

The following data is stored locally on your device using the
operating system's standard preferences storage:

- Your current chapter, completed chapters, XP, streak, and level
- Your journal reflections (free-text entries you write)
- Your progress through challenges and quiz questions
- Your sound on/off preference

You can erase all of this at any time from **Settings → Reset
All Progress**, or by uninstalling the app.

## What leaves your device

The app makes exactly **one network call** at launch: it checks
GitHub's public Releases API (`api.github.com`) to learn whether a
newer version of the app has been published. The request contains
no user identifiers, no device identifiers, no analytics, and no
telemetry. It is functionally equivalent to your web browser
fetching a public web page.

The auto-update prompt, when shown, opens the public GitHub
release page in your device's default browser.

## Third parties

We do not use third-party analytics, advertising, or crash
reporting. The only third-party network endpoint the app ever
contacts is GitHub's public API, and only to check for app
updates.

## Children

The app does not target children under 13 and does not knowingly
collect any information from children.

## Changes to this policy

If we ever change this policy in a way that affects what leaves
your device, we will note the change in the next release and ask
you to re-acknowledge it on first launch of the new version.
